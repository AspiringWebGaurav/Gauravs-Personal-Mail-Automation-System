# GPMAS — ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (AUP)

> **Effective Date:** February 2026  
> **Policy Version:** 1.0  
> **Service Provider:** Gaurav Patil  
> **Service:** Gaurav's Personal Mail Automation System (GPMAS)

---

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines the acceptable and prohibited uses of the Gaurav's Personal Mail Automation System ("GPMAS," "the Service"). This policy is designed to protect the integrity of the Service, its users, recipients of communications, and the broader internet community.

By using the Service, you agree to comply with this AUP. Violations may result in immediate suspension or termination of access, and may be reported to relevant law enforcement authorities.

---

## 2. Prohibited Usage

### 2.1 Spam and Unsolicited Communications
You may **NOT** use the Service to:

- Send unsolicited bulk emails (spam) to recipients who have not provided explicit consent
- Send mass marketing emails or promotional campaigns to purchased or harvested email lists
- Send messages that disguise or misrepresent the identity of the sender
- Send messages with deceptive subject lines designed to mislead recipients
- Engage in any form of email bombing or message flooding

### 2.2 Harassment and Abuse
You may **NOT** use the Service to:

- Send threatening, abusive, harassing, defamatory, or intimidating communications
- Stalk, bully, or engage in any form of targeted harassment against any individual
- Send repeated unwanted communications to recipients who have requested to stop receiving messages
- Engage in doxxing or the unauthorized publication of personal information

### 2.3 Illegal Communications
You may **NOT** use the Service to:

- Distribute or promote illegal content in any jurisdiction
- Facilitate, promote, or engage in any criminal activity
- Transmit content that infringes on the intellectual property rights of others
- Send communications that violate any applicable law, statute, regulation, or ordinance
- Distribute content depicting or promoting violence, exploitation, or harm
- Transmit content that incites discrimination based on race, gender, religion, nationality, disability, sexual orientation, or age

### 2.4 Phishing and Social Engineering
You may **NOT** use the Service to:

- Send phishing emails designed to deceive recipients into disclosing personal information
- Impersonate any person, organization, or entity
- Create fake login pages or deceptive links
- Engage in any form of social engineering attack
- Distribute malware, ransomware, viruses, or other harmful software

---

## 3. System Exploitation

### 3.1 Provider Quota Misuse
You may **NOT**:

- Attempt to circumvent, bypass, or manipulate email provider quotas or rate limits
- Create multiple provider configurations with the same credentials to multiply quotas
- Exploit provider rotation logic to exceed intended sending volumes
- Use the Service in any way that causes provider accounts to be suspended or terminated

### 3.2 System Integrity
You may **NOT**:

- Attempt to bypass authentication or access controls
- Exploit vulnerabilities in the system's logic, APIs, or infrastructure
- Interfere with or disrupt the operation of the Service
- Use automated tools to interact with the Service beyond normal usage patterns
- Attempt to access data, accounts, or systems that you are not authorized to access
- Modify, tamper with, or reverse engineer any aspect of the Service

### 3.3 Resource Abuse
You may **NOT**:

- Consume system resources in a manner disproportionate to normal usage
- Initiate operations designed to exhaust database quotas, API limits, or server capacity
- Use the Service for load testing, stress testing, or benchmarking without explicit authorization

## 4. Account Responsibility

### 4.1 Credential Security
You are solely responsible for:

- Maintaining the confidentiality and security of your access credentials
- All activities that occur under your authenticated session
- Immediately notifying the Owner of any unauthorized access or security breach

### 4.2 Content Responsibility
You are solely responsible for:

- All content you create, send, or distribute through the Service
- Ensuring your communications comply with all applicable laws and regulations
- Obtaining any necessary consent from recipients before sending communications
- The accuracy of the information you provide to the Service

## 5. Compliance Requirements

### 5.1 Legal Compliance
You must comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

- **Anti-Spam Laws:** CAN-SPAM Act (USA), CASL (Canada), PECR (UK), and equivalent regulations in your jurisdiction
- **Data Protection:** GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), and equivalent data protection regulations
- **Electronic Communications:** All laws governing electronic communications in your jurisdiction

### 5.2 Industry Standards
You must adhere to industry best practices for email communications, including:

- Sending only to recipients who have given consent
- Providing clear identification of the sender in all communications
- Respecting opt-out and unsubscribe requests promptly
- Maintaining accurate contact lists

### 6.1 Right to Suspend
The Owner reserves the right to immediately suspend or restrict access to the Service, without prior notice, if:

- There is a reasonable suspicion of AUP violation

### 6.2 Right to Terminate
The Owner reserves the right to permanently terminate access to the Service for:

### 6.3 Right to Report
The Owner reserves the right to report any suspected illegal activity to appropriate law enforcement authorities, and to cooperate with any resulting investigation.
