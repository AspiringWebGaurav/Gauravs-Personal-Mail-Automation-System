# GPMAS — Anti-Abuse Policy

> **Version:** 1.0 · **Classification:** Policy  
> **Last Updated:** February 2026

---

## Scope

This policy defines what constitutes abuse of the GPMAS platform and the measures in place to prevent, detect, and respond to abusive behavior.

---

## Definition of Abuse

Abuse is any use of GPMAS that violates its intended purpose, damages its reputation, harms recipients, or undermines the integrity of the email ecosystem.

---

## Prohibited Activities

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| **Spam** | Unsolicited bulk email, commercial solicitation, unwanted promotions |
| **Harassment** | Threatening, abusive, or intimidating communications |
| **Fraud** | Phishing attempts, identity theft, financial scams |
| **Illegal content** | Communications that violate applicable laws |
| **System exploitation** | Attempting to bypass quotas, circumvent rate limits, exploit provider APIs |
| **Data harvesting** | Using the invite system to collect or verify email addresses |
| **Impersonation** | Sending emails that misrepresent the sender's identity |

---

## Prevention Mechanisms

| Mechanism | How It Prevents Abuse |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **Single-owner access** | Limits system usage to one verified individual |
| **Provider quotas** | Enforces daily sending volume limits |
| **Zero-provider guard** | Prevents unsanctioned delivery pathways |
| **Invitation expiry** | Limits the active window for link-based attacks |
| **Audit trail** | Creates a complete record of all operations for accountability |
| **Emergency stop** | Enables immediate halt of all operations |

---

## Detection

Abuse may be detected through:
- Audit trail analysis revealing unusual patterns
- Provider feedback indicating delivery issues
- Recipient complaints reaching the system owner
- Burn Monitor indicating unsustainable usage patterns

---

## Enforcement

If abuse is confirmed:
1. **Immediate cessation** — the system owner must stop the abusive activity
2. **Investigation** — audit trail provides complete diagnostic context
3. **Corrective action** — provider credentials may be rotated; affected data may be purged
4. **Prevention** — additional controls may be implemented to prevent recurrence
