## üìê Documentation Design Principles

**Why is documentation deeply structured?**  
Shallow documentation creates ambiguity. Each document covers one topic with layered depth (what ‚Üí why ‚Üí how ‚Üí failures ‚Üí business implications ‚Üí boundaries). This makes information findable and reviewable independently.

**Why are folders separated?**  
Separation of concerns. Legal documents are reviewed by legal teams, not engineers. Architecture documents are reviewed by architects, not investors. Folder boundaries create natural access paths for different audiences.

**Why is the open-source strategy buried?**  
It is intentional. GPMAS is proprietary software. The open-source strategy is an exploratory future consideration, not a current direction. It is accessible for strategic review but not promoted to avoid misrepresentation of the project's licensing status.

**Why are legal files segmented?**  
Each legal document serves a distinct function: Terms govern usage, Privacy governs data, License governs IP, AUP governs behavior. Segmentation enables targeted review, targeted updates, and clear legal responsibility boundaries.
